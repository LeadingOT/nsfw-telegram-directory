# What is Comp Analysis

**Comp analysis, short for comparative market analysis, is a method used to determine a property's market value by comparing it to recently sold, similar properties in the same area.**

A comp analysis is a foundational due diligence process in real estate, performed by agents, investors, and appraisers. It involves identifying a subject property's key attributes—such as square footage, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, lot size, age, condition, and location—and then finding a set of "comps," or comparable properties, that closely match these traits. The most critical comps are properties that have sold very recently (typically within the last 3-6 months) within a close geographic radius, as they reflect the most current market conditions. Adjustments are then made to the sale prices of these comps to account for any significant differences with the subject property, arriving at an estimated value range.

This process is distinct from a formal appraisal but serves a similar initial purpose. While an appraisal is a certified valuation for a lender, a comp analysis is often an agent's tool to advise clients on pricing strategy. For example, an agent preparing to list a 3-bedroom, 2-bathroom home in a specific suburb would gather data on 5-10 similar homes that sold in that neighborhood over the past few months, noting final sale prices, days on market, and any premium features like a renovated kitchen.

Comp analysis matters because it grounds pricing in objective market data rather than speculation. For sellers, it informs a competitive listing price to attract buyers. For buyers, it provides a benchmark to make a strong, justified offer and avoid overpaying. For real estate investors, it is essential for calculating potential profit, such as estimating the after-repair value (ARV) of a fixer-upper by comparing it to updated homes. Inaccurate comp analysis can lead to a property languishing on the market or money being left on the table.

**Related terms:** Automated Valuation Model (AVM), Broker Price Opinion (BPO), Appraisal, After Repair Value (ARV).
