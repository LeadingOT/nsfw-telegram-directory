# What is Transaction Coordination

**Transaction Coordination is the administrative and procedural management of all tasks, communications, and documentation required to move a real estate deal from a ratified contract to a successful closing.**

This specialized role acts as the project manager for a real estate transaction, ensuring that every contingency, deadline, and requirement outlined in the purchase agreement is met in the correct order and on time. The coordinator does not provide legal advice or negotiate terms, but instead implements the agreement by meticulously tracking key dates (like inspection and financing deadlines), facilitating communication between all parties (agents, lenders, title companies, inspectors, and attorneys), and collecting and distributing necessary documents. Their primary goal is to create a seamless, efficient process that minimizes errors and prevents deals from falling apart due to administrative oversights.

In practice, a transaction coordinator manages a detailed checklist unique to each deal. For example, upon contract ratification, they would immediately ensure the earnest money deposit is received, send the contract to the lender and title company, schedule the home inspection, and remind the buyer's agent of upcoming deadlines. They follow up daily with the lender on loan underwriting status, coordinate the appraisal, ensure the title search is completed, and prepare the closing disclosure for review. Ultimately, they work to get all parties to the closing table with all paperwork correctly executed.

This function matters because modern real estate transactions involve a complex web of interdependent tasks. A single missed deadline can terminate a contract or cause significant financial penalty. By centralizing oversight, transaction coordination reduces stress for agents, increases transaction velocity, improves compliance, and enhances the client experience through clear, consistent communication. It allows real estate agents to focus on client service and lead generation rather than administrative details.
