# What is NOI (Net Operating Income)

**Net Operating Income (NOI) is a key real estate profitability metric that calculates a property's annual income generated from operations after subtracting all necessary operating expenses.**

NOI represents the property's potential cash flow before accounting for financing costs (like mortgage payments) and capital expenditures. It is calculated using a straightforward formula: **NOI = Gross Operating Income – Operating Expenses**. Gross Operating Income includes all revenue from the property, primarily rental income, but also parking fees, service charges, and vending machine income. Operating Expenses encompass the costs required to run and maintain the asset, such as property management fees, insurance, property taxes, utilities, repairs, and maintenance. Critically, NOI excludes mortgage payments, income taxes, depreciation, and capital improvements (like a new roof), as these are not considered core operational costs.

This metric is fundamental because it isolates the property's operational efficiency, allowing for an "apples-to-apples" comparison between different assets regardless of their financing structure. A consistently high or growing NOI indicates a well-managed, income-producing property. For example, a commercial building with $500,000 in annual rental income and $200,000 in operating expenses has an NOI of $300,000. This figure is then used as the foundation for several critical analyses in real estate investment.

NOI is indispensable for valuation and investment decision-making. It is the key input for the capitalization rate (cap rate) calculation, where **Property Value = NOI / Cap Rate**. Investors use NOI to assess a property's ability to service debt (via the Debt Service Coverage Ratio) and to project returns. On realestateai.tools, you'll find analytical and financial modeling tools that automate NOI calculations, forecast scenarios by adjusting income and expenses, and help benchmark a property's performance against market averages. Understanding NOI is essential for evaluating any income-producing real estate asset.

**Related Terms:** Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate), Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR), Cash Flow, Gross Operating Income, Operating Expenses.
