# How to Give a Startup Offer: The Complete Guide

## The 3-Minute Offer Conversation Framework

### Opening (30 seconds)
**Set positive tone + context**

> "Hey [Name], I'm excited to share some good news. We'd love to have you join the team. I want to walk you through the offer details and make sure you have everything you need to make a decision."

**Key:** Start with enthusiasm, not numbers. You're inviting them to join a mission, not just presenting a spreadsheet.

---

## The Comp Package Breakdown (2 minutes)

### 1. Base Salary (30 seconds)
**Don't just say the number. Frame it.**

❌ **Bad:** "Your base salary is $150,000."

✅ **Good:** "For base salary, we're offering **$150,000**, which is **right at the median** for Senior Engineers at Series A companies in SF based on our benchmark data."

**Talk Points:**
- "This is in the **[P25/P50/P75]** percentile for [role] at [stage] companies"
- "We reviewed compensation data from [data sources] to ensure this is competitive"
- If below market: "We're slightly below FAANG, but here's why: [equity upside/faster growth/impact]"
- If at/above market: "This puts you in the **top [X]%** of offers for this role"

### 2. Equity (60 seconds)
**This is where most founders fumble. Equity needs translation.**

❌ **Bad:** "You're getting 50,000 shares."

✅ **Good:** "You're getting **50,000 shares**, which is **0.15% of the company**. At our current valuation of $50M, that's worth **$75,000 today**. If we hit our Series B milestones and reach a $200M valuation, your equity would be worth **$300,000**."

**Talk Points:**
- **Always give the percentage:** "That's **[X]%** of the company"
- **Translate to dollars:** "At today's valuation, that's worth $[X]"
- **Show upside scenarios:** "If we [milestone], that could be worth $[X]"
- **Compare to benchmarks:** "For a [role] at [stage], typical equity is [0.X%-0.Y%]. We're offering **[above/at/slightly below]** that."
- **Vesting schedule:** "This vests over 4 years with a 1-year cliff, which is standard"

**Equity Scenario Table (show this visually):**
```
Current valuation:   $50M  → Your equity worth: $75,000
Series B (2 years):  $200M → Your equity worth: $300,000
Exit (4 years):      $500M → Your equity worth: $750,000
```

### 3. Bonus/Benefits (30 seconds)
**Keep it simple, but don't skip it.**

✅ **Talk Points:**
- "Target bonus is **[10-20%]** of base, tied to [company/individual] performance"
- "Full benefits: health/dental/vision, 401k, [unlimited PTO/4 weeks PTO]"
- "We also cover [laptop/home office/learning budget]"

## Handling the "Is This Competitive?" Question

### If They Ask: "How does this compare to other offers?"

**Framework: Acknowledge → Benchmark → Differentiate**

✅ **Response:**

> "Great question. Let me break it down:
> 
> **Base salary**: You're at the **[50th/75th] percentile** for [role] at [stage] companies in [city]. That's **[$X-$Y range]** based on H1B data and Carta benchmarks.
> 
> **Equity**: At **[X]%**, you're **[above/at]** the typical range of **[Y-Z]%** for this role and stage.
> 
> **Total comp**: Base + equity value today = **$[X]**. In 2 years, if we hit our milestones, total comp could be **$[Y]**.
> 
> Here's why we structure it this way: [we're earlier stage / we prioritize equity upside / we're capital-efficient]."

## The 5 Most Common Negotiation Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "I was hoping for $[X] base"
**When they ask for 10-20% more**

✅ **Response:**
> "I totally get it. Here's where we're at: Our budget for this role is **$[Y]**, which is at the **[P50/P75]** for [role] at [stage] companies. 
> 
> I have a bit of room—I could go to **$[Y + $5-10K]**, but I can't hit $[X] without going above our comp structure.
> 
> Can I ask: is the gap mostly about the base, or is it total comp? Because we have more flexibility on equity."

**Key:** Show you've done homework (benchmarks), show small flexibility, redirect to equity.

### Scenario 2: "FAANG is offering me $[much higher]"
**When competing with Big Tech**

✅ **Response:**
> "Congrats on the FAANG offer—that's awesome. You're right, we can't match a $[X] base. Here's how I think about it:
> 
> **FAANG**: Higher cash, lower equity upside, bigger company dynamics
> **Us**: Base is **$[Y]** (which is **[P50/P75]** for startups), but **[X]%** equity. If we hit our targets, that equity could be worth **$[Z]** in 2-3 years.
> 
> The real question is: do you want the **guaranteed cash** now, or do you want **ownership + upside** in something you're building?
> 
> No wrong answer—just different paths."

**Key:** Don't compete on cash. Compete on mission + equity upside + impact.

### Scenario 3: "Can I get more equity instead of cash?"
**When they're equity-motivated (GREAT sign)**

✅ **Response:**
> "Love that you're thinking long-term. Yes, we can shift comp toward equity.
> 
> Here's the math: If we reduce base by **$[X]**, I can increase your equity to **[Y%]** (up from [Z%]). At today's valuation, that's an extra **$[A]**, but if we hit Series B milestones, that's worth **$[B]**.
> 
> Does that trade-off make sense for you?"

**Key:** Show the math clearly. Make sure they understand the risk/reward.

### Scenario 4: "I need [X] weeks to decide"
**When they're stalling**

✅ **Response:**
> "Totally fair. Can I ask: what would help you get to a decision faster? Is it:
> - More info about the role/team/company?
> - Talking to other team members?
> - Comparing to other offers?
> - Something else?
> 
> I want to give you the time you need, but I also need to keep our hiring process moving. Could we aim for a decision by **[1 week from now]**? If you need more time, just let me know why and I'll see what I can do."

**Key:** Uncover the real blocker. Set a soft deadline.

### Scenario 5: "This equity percentage seems low"
**When they've done research (or talked to Twitter)**

✅ **Response:**
> "Let me show you how we calculated it:
> 
> For a **[role]** at a **[stage]** company, the typical range is **[X%-Y%]** according to Carta data. We're offering **[Z%]**, which is **[at/above/slightly below]** that.
> 
> Here's why: [we're post-Series A so percentages are smaller / we already have 50 employees / valuations compressed the pool].
> 
> If the percentage feels small, let's look at the **dollar value**: at our current $[X]M valuation, your [Z%] is worth $[A]. If we 4x to $[Y]M, that's $[B].
> 
> Does that help clarify the actual value?"

**Key:** Benchmarks + translate to dollars + show scenarios.

## Mistakes That Kill Offers

### ❌ Mistake 1: Not explaining equity
**Bad:** "You're getting 50,000 shares."
**Why it fails:** Candidate has no idea if that's good or bad.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Anchoring too low
**Bad:** "What's your current salary?" → "Okay, we can do 10% more."
**Why it fails:** You're basing comp on their old (possibly underpaid) job, not market rate.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Being defensive about comp
**Bad:** "This is all we can afford."
**Why it fails:** Sounds like the company is broke.
**Better:** "This is our comp structure for this role based on benchmarks."

### ❌ Mistake 4: Negotiating over email
**Bad:** Going back-and-forth on numbers via email.
**Why it fails:** Tone gets lost, candidate feels like a transaction.
**Better:** "Let's hop on a quick call to discuss."

### ❌ Mistake 5: Not having a walk-away number
**Bad:** Saying yes to every ask because you're desperate.
**Why it fails:** Breaks your comp structure, sets bad precedent.
**Better:** Know your max before the conversation.

## Pre-Offer Checklist

Before you make the offer call, have these ready:

### Numbers
- [ ] Base salary: $[X] (at P[25/50/75] benchmark)
- [ ] Equity: [X]% / [Y] shares
- [ ] Equity value today: $[X]
- [ ] Equity value at [milestone]: $[Y]
- [ ] Target bonus: [X]%
- [ ] Total comp today: $[X]
- [ ] Total comp at milestone: $[Y]

### Talking Points
- [ ] Why this comp is competitive (cite benchmarks)
- [ ] 2-3 equity scenarios (current / Series B / exit)
- [ ] Your max (what's your walk-away number?)
- [ ] Non-comp selling points (mission, team, growth, impact)

### Backup Plan
- [ ] If they ask for more base: can you go to $[X]?
- [ ] If they want more equity: can you shift comp mix?
- [ ] If they're comparing to FAANG: what's your pitch?

## The Close

**After you've presented the offer and answered questions:**

✅ **Closing Script:**

> "So that's the full picture: **$[X] base**, **[Y]% equity** (worth $[Z] today, potentially $[A] at Series B), plus benefits.
> 
> I know this is a big decision. Here's what I need from you:
> 
> 1. **Timeline**: Can you let me know by **[date]** if you're leaning yes or if you have concerns?
> 2. **Questions**: What else do you need from me to make this decision?
> 3. **Transparency**: If there's a gap between this offer and what you need, tell me now so we can talk through it.
> 
> I really want this to work. What's your gut saying?"

**Key:** Set expectations, invite concerns, show you care.

## Remember

**The best offers are:**
1. **Clear** — No confusion about numbers
2. **Competitive** — Backed by real benchmarks
3. **Compelling** — Show the upside, not just the cash
4. **Conversational** — Not a negotiation, a partnership discussion
5. **Collaborative** — "How do we make this work?" not "Take it or leave it"

**Your goal:** Make them feel valued, informed, and excited—not pressured.
